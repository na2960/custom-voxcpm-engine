The electron itself is described by a wave-like quantity whose curvature in space determines its kinetic behavior, while the repeating arrangement of atoms creates a repeating landscape that continuously influences that wave, so the governing relationship balances spatial curvature against a periodically repeating environment. Because any repeating structure can be decomposed into a collection of simple repeating spatial rhythms, the electron wave becomes coupled only to rhythms compatible with the crystal's spacing, producing ranges of allowed collective motions and ranges where no self-consistent motion can exist, which are the mathematical origin of energy bands and band gaps.
