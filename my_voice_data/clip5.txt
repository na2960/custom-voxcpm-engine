Materials behave as metals when electrons can reorganize themselves within partially available bands, while semiconductors possess a small forbidden region between occupied and unoccupied bands, making their electrical behavior highly sensitive to temperature, light, impurities, and externally applied fields. This is useful for engineering because it provides a direct connection between atomic arrangement and macroscopic behavior, allowing materials and devices to be designed with specific conductive, insulating, optical, sensing, computing, and energy-conversion properties by controlling how electrons are permitted or forbidden to organize themselves within the crystal.
